# Common Bond: The First 30 Days Roadmap

**Now that your ACN is active and your Trust ABN is reactivating, use this checklist to ensure your infrastructure matches the "Doctor-Developer" quality described in your business plan.**

This roadmap is designed to move Common Bond from a legal entity to an operational vehicle capable of supporting the "Receptor" platform deployment.

---

## 1. Financial & Tax Foundation
*Establishing the "Shared Bond" of financial integrity.*

- [ ] **Open the Trust Bank Account**: Visit your bank with the Original 2022 Deed and the 2026 Appointment Deed. Account name should be: *Common Bond Pty Ltd ATF The Ammendolea Family Trust*.
- [ ] **Set up Accounting Software**: Register for Xero or QuickBooks. Link your Trust bank feed. This is where you will manage your GST obligations and track "Receptor" R&D costs.
- [ ] **Initial Capital Injection**: Deposit the $1.00 "Initial Sum" from the Settlor and your own seed capital into the Trust account to officially "fund" the business.
- [ ] **Tax Professional Alignment**: Discuss with your accountant the requirement for a "Bucket Company" (Optional for later) to hold profits that you don't want to distribute to individuals this year.

---

## 2. IP & Governance (The Firewall)
*Protecting the intellectual core of the "Doctor-Developer" advantage.*

- [ ] **Execute the IP License Agreement**: Sign a formal document where *MyJMO Pty Ltd* licenses the "Receptor" software to the *Ammendolea Family Trust*.
- [ ] **Register the Domain**: Secure `commonbond.com.au` or similar. Ensure it is owned by the Trust.
- [ ] **Professional Service Agreement (PSA)**: Draft your standard consulting contract. As an agile consultancy, ensure your PSA has clear "Out of Scope" clauses to prevent the "Feature Creep" mentioned in your [SWOT Analysis](https://docs.commonbond.au/corporate/docs/business-planning/strategy-vision/swot).
- [ ] **SaaS Terms of Service (ToS)**: Draft the user agreement for Receptor. Crucially, include a **Data Sovereignty Clause** stating all medical workforce data is stored strictly in Australia.
